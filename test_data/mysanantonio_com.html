American Kings are for real. So are the screams.

The powerpop and rock ‘n’ roll band led by teenage brothers and Churchill High School students Jonathan “Stone” Morales and Jude Morales roared early Saturday night at Picks Bar for an equally young audience.

Imagine a much younger version of the now-defunct Australian rock band Jet and you’ve got American Kings (on Twitter @KingsAmerican).

This is not just the new Zots, the Morales brothers two-man band. Bassist James Guerra and guitarist Grant Johnson are genuine rock stars and add a new toughness and skinny jean visual appeal. All of them sing.

On Saturday, young fans at the all-ages gig gathered at the foot of the stage and squealed with delight every time Stone or Grant moved too close to the edge. When they bent down with their guitars, boys and girls reached up to touch the boys and their instruments.

Jude Morales is the secret ingredient with a stomping style that often focuses on floor-tom and bass-drum thunder for the powerpop that owes equal debts to AC/DC, the Ramones, the Knack and Jimmy Eat World. Guerra also drives the music and adds swagger.

American Kings rocked out on originals “Love You More (Say It),” “I Get Crazy,” “Kill Me With Your Kiss,” “Never Want This Day to End” and “Pretty Good Friday Night,” as well as covers of the Knack’s “My Sharona,” Jet’s “Are You Gonna be My Girl,” Walk the Moon’s “Shut Up and Dance with Me” and the Ramones’ “Blitzkrieg Bop,” the latter as an encore after a brief onstage huddle session.

The band is managed by Michael Morales (Morales is the Grammy-winning producer and ’80s rock musician), and a recent trip to the West Coast is reported to have been promising with record labels and publishing companies expressing interest in American Kings.
